A cool thing that started when i really started playing guitar was that i also started being very attentive to the guitar in music. This is probably a reason why music with a good/melodic guitar is usally my favorite to listen to.

I like to play guitar from time to time. But I dislike that if I dont play guitar for a while my skill-level goes down, so i try to practice and play every other day. Its tough though since there is a lot of other things that comes up and it can get annoying sometimes lol. Guitar is pretty much a side hobby/skill for me. And recently i have barley touched it.

I Love playing itchicka Nitos songs . I used to know quite a lot of songs from him. It was my favorite to learn his songs; from simpler to harder as my skill lever progressed. However sadly i have forgotten quite a bit of songs by him.. I am still keeping up and reviewing my favorite songs though, i never want to forget playing these songs. My favorite to play by him is "Guitar riff to flex at home party"- its so quick and so fun to play! I have the habit of skimming trough the parts i struggle with so i never really learn to play them 100% all the time. Even though ive known this song for about 3+ years now it still feels so fresh and new!
My favorite to play on guitar is challenging pieces where i truly get a feeling that i want to master it- or at least to make it simpler. Its those songs just above my skil level that i look forward to play. I like that steel focus i get when i try to play a part again and again, feeling my fingers starting to cramp up- my mind so focused that it feels like my conciousnes goes away. I like a good hour of focus on guitar where i can feel challenged- the feeling afterwards is like after a good workout.
Making melodies is annoying. ! I find it a somewhat daunting, tiering and long task to try to construct a piece my my own hand. When i make a song i end up playing a piece in it again and again where i end up spending suprisingly a lot of time. I find it annoying to spend so much time on seemingly nothing and i end up feeling tiered by it afterwards. I do still jam though; I have to try and see whatever comes up to my mind, and i see melody-making as a slow, side process that i can pick up whenever. When i play guitar i tend to lean on doing what i want the most though; playing and learning challenging things to inprove my level.However, its exiting to exstend myself and exsplore that part of creativity though.
